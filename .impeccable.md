## Design Context

### Users
People traveling together who need to keep a lightweight shared ledger during a trip. They are usually mobile-first, moving quickly, and using the app in real contexts like restaurants, transport, lodging, and final trip wrap-up. Their main job is to split trip expenses with minimal friction and settle balances cleanly when the trip ends.

### Brand Personality
Modern, easy, friendly. The product should feel approachable and low-stress rather than technical, cold, or intimidating. It should help groups resolve money moments smoothly without making the experience feel heavy or financial-infrastructure-like.

### Aesthetic Direction
Contemporary consumer fintech with a travel-social bias. Clear hierarchy, fast comprehension, and lightweight warmth matter more than aggressive futurism or "premium dark protocol" theatrics. The experience should feel simple enough for casual group use and polished enough to be trusted with shared expenses. It should not feel intimidating, militarized, cyberpunk, or overly enterprise.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize fast understanding over stylistic intensity.
2. Make trip coordination feel lightweight, social, and low-friction.
3. Use visual emphasis to guide the next action, not to perform brand attitude.
4. Keep trust signals calm and legible instead of dramatic.
5. Design for mobile, distracted, in-the-moment use.
